## Implementation Plan for Next Session

### New Domain: `health:*`

Create three foundational skills as integrated system:

#### 1. `health:sleep-routine`

**Framework grounding:**
- Matthew Walker's "Why We Sleep"
- Sleep hygiene research (NSF guidelines)
- Circadian rhythm science
- Andrew Huberman's sleep protocols

**Core phases:**
- Wind-down ritual (90-60 min before bed)
- Sleep environment optimization
- Consistent schedule (same bed/wake times)
- Morning wake protocol (light exposure, delay caffeine)
- Recovery from bad nights

**Key features:**
- Pairs with daily-shutdown (work closure â†’ sleep prep)
- Pairs with daily-startup (wake protocol â†’ work mode)
- Tracking: sleep quality, duration, wake feeling
- Disruption handling (travel, stress, etc.)

**Common pitfalls:**
- "Just one more episode/chapter" (no hard stop time)
- Inconsistent weekends (social jet lag)
- Phone in bed (blue light + infinite scroll)
- Caffeine too late (6-hour half-life)

**Length target:** 200-300 lines

#### 2. `health:strength-program`

**Framework grounding:**
- Progressive overload principle (Selye, Zatsiorsky)
- Evidence-based programs (5/3/1, Starting Strength, GZCLP)
- Recovery science (Schoenfeld, Mike Israetel)
- Simplicity (compound movements, linear progression)

**Core phases:**
- Program selection (match to experience level)
- Workout tracking (sets, reps, weight progression)
- Progressive overload (when/how to add weight)
- Deload weeks (planned recovery)
- Plateau handling (reset, switch program, etc.)

**Key features:**
- Integrates with sleep-routine (recovery is training)
- Integrates with nutrition (fuel for performance)
- Tracking template (simple spreadsheet or app)
- Travel/disruption protocols (maintenance vs. progression)

**Common pitfalls:**
- Program hopping (never seeing results)
- No tracking (guessing what you lifted last time)
- Ego lifting (form breaks down for heavier weight)
- No deloads (burn out, injury risk)
- Ignoring recovery (sleep, nutrition, stress)

**Length target:** 250-350 lines (includes program templates)

---

#### 3. `health:nutrition-planning`

**Framework grounding:**
- Evidence-based macros (ISSN, Examine.com)
- Meal prep systems (batch cooking, containers)
- Flexible dieting (80/20, sustainable not perfect)
- Dr. Mike Israetel's "Diet Periodization"

**Core phases:**
- Calculate needs (TDEE, macro targets)
- Meal planning (weekly system)
- Meal prep workflow (shopping â†’ cooking â†’ storage)
- Tracking (app-based or simple rules)
- Adjustment protocol (not losing/gaining as expected)

**Key features:**
- Integrates with strength-program (fuel training)
- Simple rules option (for those who hate tracking)
- Disruption handling (eating out, travel, social events)
- Template meals (proven combinations that work)

**Common pitfalls:**
- Perfectionism (all-or-nothing thinking)
- Not tracking protein (most important macro)
- Cutting too aggressively (lose muscle, tank energy)
- No meal prep (reactive daily decisions fail)
- Ignoring hunger/fullness cues (override body signals)

**Length target:** 250-350 lines
